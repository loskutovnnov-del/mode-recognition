With the introduction of modern active elements and devices into electrical networks (flexible transmission lines, controlled shunt reactors, energy storage units, distributed generation sources, etc.), traditional methods for recognizing fault conditions for the correct operation of relay protection and automation (RPA) devices sometimes become ineffective. With the development and widespread adoption of the IEC 61850 standard and simulation software packages, access is becoming available to a large volume of information about electrical network conditions, enabling the development of fundamentally new algorithms for RPA, including those based on machine learning methods. A new approach to fault recognition on transmission lines with branches is being explored through the simultaneous analysis of several information features and the application of group machine learning algorithms: decision tree, random forest, and gradient boosting. Simulation modeling and the Monte Carlo method are used to obtain training samples. Testing of the studied methods demonstrated the required flexibility, the ability to use a large number of information parameters (multiparameter), and improved fault recognition results compared to remote relay protection devices. The implementation of the decision tree algorithm for microprocessor-based relay protection and automation devices does not require hardware upgrades, but rather involves the modification of specialized software.
